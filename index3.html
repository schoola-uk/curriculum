About Schoola

Schoola (schoola.uk) is a free, independent curriculum signposting tool for UK primary school pupils aged 5–11 and their parents.

We map the full statutory National Curriculum (Key Stage 1 and Key Stage 2) and curate links to the best free educational resources — videos from BBC Teach and Oak National Academy, printables from Twinkl and BBC Bitesize, and interactive quizzes.

Schoola does not create or host its own content. We simply make it easier to find what already exists.

❓ Frequently asked questions
What Maths topics should Year 1 children know? ＋
Year 1 Maths covers counting to 100, reading and writing numbers to 20, place value (tens and ones), number bonds to 10 and 20, simple addition and subtraction, counting in 2s/5s/10s, halves and quarters, measuring length and height, telling time to o'clock and half past, and recognising coins and notes.
What times tables do children learn in KS1? ＋
In KS1 (Years 1–2), children learn the 2, 5, and 10 times tables. By the end of Year 2, they should be able to recall and use these fluently. Times tables for 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, and 12 are introduced in KS2.
How can I help my child with KS1 Maths at home? ＋
Use everyday opportunities — counting fruit, sharing food equally, paying with coins, reading the clock, or sorting objects by shape and size. Short, regular practice (10 minutes a day) is more effective than long sessions. BBC Bitesize and Twinkl both offer free worksheets and games for KS1 Maths.

❓ Frequently asked questions
What English skills do children need for KS2 SATs? ＋
KS2 English SATs (Year 6) assess reading comprehension, grammar, punctuation, and spelling. Key areas include inference and deduction, vocabulary in context, subordinate clauses, apostrophes, commas, and the Year 5–6 statutory spelling list. BBC Bitesize has free SATs revision resources for all these areas.
How is KS2 English different from KS1? ＋
KS2 English builds significantly on KS1. Children move from simple sentences and phonics to complex multi-paragraph writing, inference in reading, formal and informal registers, and sophisticated grammar (relative clauses, modal verbs, passive voice). Spelling patterns become more complex, including prefixes, suffixes, and etymology.

❓ Frequently asked questions
What science topics are covered in KS1? ＋
KS1 Science (Years 1–2) covers: everyday materials and their properties, plants (identifying common plants, what they need to grow), animals including humans (carnivores, herbivores, omnivores; basic needs), living things and their habitats, food chains, and seasonal changes (the four seasons).
What science topics are covered in KS2? ＋
KS2 Science (Years 3–6) covers: rocks and fossils, plants (pollination, seed dispersal), light and shadows, the human digestive system, states of matter, sound, electricity and circuits, life cycles, the Solar System, forces and gravity, properties and changes of materials, classification, the circulatory system, evolution, light (refraction), and electricity (voltage).

The British school curriculum
explained for parents

The British (England) primary school curriculum is set by the Department for Education (DfE) and is followed by all state schools. It covers Key Stage 1 (Years 1–2, ages 5–7) and Key Stage 2 (Years 3–6, ages 7–11). Schoola maps every statutory topic so you can follow exactly what your child is learning.

🏫 How the British primary curriculum is structured
📗
Key Stage 1 (KS1)
Years 1 and 2, ages 5–7. Core subjects: English and Maths. Foundation subjects: Science, History, Geography, Art, DT, Computing, Music, PE, RE, and PSHE.
Ages 5–7
📘
Key Stage 2 (KS2)
Years 3–6, ages 7–11. Same subjects as KS1 but with greater depth and complexity. Year 6 pupils sit national SATs assessments in English and Maths.
Ages 7–11
📋
National Curriculum
The statutory National Curriculum is set by the DfE. All state-maintained schools in England must follow it. Academy schools are not legally required to but most do.
DfE statutory
📊
Assessment
Children are assessed at the end of KS1 (Year 2) and KS2 (Year 6). KS2 SATs in English (reading, SPAG) and Maths are externally marked. KS1 teacher assessment is used to report progress.
SATs & teacher assessment

📋 Core vs Foundation subjects
⭐
Core subjects
English, Maths, and Science are the three core subjects of the UK National Curriculum. They receive more curriculum time and are assessed at the end of KS1 and KS2.
English · Maths · Science
🎨
Foundation subjects
History, Geography, Art & Design, Design & Technology, Computing, Music, PE, RE, and PSHE make up the foundation subjects. All are statutory.
9 foundation subjects
🗓️
Curriculum time
Schools decide how to allocate time. Core subjects typically take up around 50–60% of the timetable. Foundation subjects share the remaining time.
School discretion
